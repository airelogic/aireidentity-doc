    Getting Started

    Learn the basics of logging into AireIdentity and its home page

    Introduction

    AireIdentity is AireInnovate’s identity management platform used to provide authentication access to the core product suite such as AireForms and Aireframe.

    First, navigate to https://identity.aireinnovate.com by clicking on this link or typing the URL into your browser.

    Log into your account using the username and password you originally signed in with when registering for Aire Identity. If you have forgotten your password then click on the “Forgotten your password?” link and this will guide you through the steps to reset your password. If you still have issues logging in then please contact support@aireinnovate.com where one of the support teams will be able to assist you.

    If you are presented with a tenant environment selection, choose the tenant and environment you wish to log in to (e.g. University of Sheffield - Production), then press continue.

    Note: You will only see this screen if you have multiple tenants or environments assigned to your account (e.g Production, Staging and Development)

    Once logged in you will be presented with a list of AireSuite applications that you can launch

    To access the tenant management screens for Aire Identity click on the ‘Tenant’ link located at the top of the screen next to the Aire Identity logo.

    Once clicked you will see information about the Tenant along with a selection of Tabs showing ‘Users, Roles, Policies, Products and Providers’.

    • Users - This is where you can add / edit / delete users in the tenant you are logged into.
    • Roles - This is where you can add / edit / delete roles in the tenant you are logged into.
    • Policies - This is where you add / edit / delete new policies to be applied against roles.
    • Products - This is where a list of products are shown that are linked to the tenant e.g. AireForms, Airframe, AireGlu etc.
    • Providers - Identity providers are used to authenticate users in your tenant. You can add and configure which identity providers are allowed for your users when logging in (e.g. Google / Azure / SAML2)
